# Artifactual: A Taxonomy of Generative Friction

Welcome to the digital archive of my attempts to negotiate with silicon-based "artists." This repository documents the volatile journey from "I can't believe a machine drew this" to "I can't believe I asked for this."

A digital purgatory for AI-generated artifacts. From prompt-engineered masterpieces to Eldritch horrors with thirteen fingers.

## The Hierarchy of Regret (The Taxonomy)

I have sorted these images into four distinct tiers of competence (or lack thereof).

### Tier 1: The Ascended
* **Status:** "I can't believe it's not human."
* **Description:** Rare alignments of prompt and parameters. Lighting is coherent, anatomy is standard-issue (five fingers per hand), and the subject doesn't look like it’s screaming internally. These are the accidents of competence.

### Tier 2: The Functional (The Uncanny)
* **Status:** "Something is rotten in the state of Pixels."
* **Description:** Looks like a masterpiece from a distance. Up close, you realize the protagonist has three rows of teeth or is wearing a shirt made of their own skin. Functional, but devoid of a soul.

### Tier 3: The Corporate NPCs
* **Status:** "The Beige Tier."
* **Description:** The result of over-sanitized safety filters and uninspired models. It’s a woman holding a coffee cup in a sunlit office. It’s so boring it’s offensive. The "Corporate Memphis" of AI.

### Tier 4: The Unholy (Digital Crimes)
* **Status:** "Call an Exorcist."
* **Description:** Total prompt divorce. Physics is a suggestion. A "Cat on a Bike" became a bicycle made of feline limbs. These stay in the repository purely as legal evidence against the developers.

## The Culprits (The Tech Stack)

* **DALL-E 3 / ChatGPT:** The Overachiever. Great at art, terrible at boundaries.
* **Bing Designer:** The Prude. Will generate a sunset but flags "a sandwich" as potentially offensive content.
* **Canva Magic Media:** The Intern. It’s trying its best with the limited resources it’s been given.
* **Craiyon:** The Chaos Engine. For when you want your images to look like they were painted with radioactive sludge in 2004.
* **Leonardo.ai / SeaArt:** The Credit-Hungry. High quality, until you run out of daily tokens and it starts drawing stick figures.
